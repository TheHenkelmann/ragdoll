Onboarding Checklist for New Employees - Lumen Analytics GmbH

This checklist guides the first 30 days and complements the employee handbook.
It is internal and is worked through by the team lead together with the new
colleague. The goal is for every new hire to feel productive and welcome by the
end of the first month, without being overwhelmed in the first week.

Day 1 - Arriving and Access
- Receive the laptop and verify that full-disk encryption is enabled
- Set up the company account and activate multi-factor authentication
- Install the password manager and store the first set of credentials
- Meet your mentor and schedule a first recurring one-on-one
- Take an office tour and get introduced to the team
- Read the employee handbook and the IT security policy
- Confirm payroll and emergency-contact details with the People team

Week 1 - Foundations
- Introduction to the Lumen Insights Platform and the product
- Complete the mandatory IT security training (passwords, VPN, data classes)
- Get access to the relevant repositories and project boards
- Set up the home-office workspace and request the equipment budget
- Submit a first small contribution or pull request
- Have a short coffee chat with someone from another team
- Review the team's working agreements and meeting rhythm

Weeks 2 to 4 - Becoming Independent
- Take ownership of a first project or ticket that can be finished quickly
- Join at least one customer call or review as an observer
- Walk through the travel and expense process in the travel portal once
- Test a vacation request in the self-service portal
- Pair with at least two different teammates to learn the codebase
- Give feedback on the onboarding so it can be improved for the next hire

After 30 Days - Feedback
- First structured feedback conversation with the team lead
- Collect and clarify any open questions from onboarding
- Confirm that all required accesses and tools are working
- Complete the onboarding checklist together and file it
- Agree on goals and a focus area for the next quarter

Tips
- Ask questions early; nobody expects you to know everything in week one.
- Write down anything that confused you - it is the best source of
  documentation improvements.
- Block focus time in your calendar so meetings do not fill the whole day.
