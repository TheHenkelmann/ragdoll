# Employee Handbook - Lumen Analytics GmbH

This handbook summarizes the most important organizational rules for everyone
working at Lumen Analytics GmbH. It is aimed at the internal workforce, is
classified as internal, and is reviewed and updated twice a year by the People
team. Where this handbook touches on technical topics such as remote-work
security, the IT Security Policy takes precedence.

## Working Hours and Flextime

The regular working week is 40 hours. We work in a flextime model with a core
window from 10:00 to 15:00. During core hours everyone should generally be
reachable, for example for meetings, reviews, or pair-programming sessions.
Outside the core window people are free to arrange their day as suits them best,
as long as commitments to the team are met.

Overtime is recorded on a working-time account and can be compensated as time
off rather than paid out. A maximum of 40 plus-hours may be carried over per
quarter; anything beyond that should be discussed with the team lead so that
workloads can be rebalanced before they become a problem.

## Remote Work and Mobile Working

Employees may work from home on up to three days per week. The remaining two
days take place in the office to encourage teamwork and spontaneous exchange.
To set up a home workspace, there is a one-time equipment budget of 750 euros
that can be spent on a desk, chair, or monitor.

Anyone who wants to work remotely for more than three days per week on a
permanent basis needs the approval of their team lead. The technical security
requirements for working outside the office - in particular the mandatory use
of the company VPN - are defined in the IT Security Policy and are binding.
Working from a different country for longer periods may have tax and legal
implications and must be cleared with the People team in advance.

## Vacation and Absences

Every full-time employee is entitled to 30 vacation days per calendar year.
Vacation is requested through the internal self-service portal and must be
approved by the team lead. Remaining vacation can be carried over until March
31 of the following year; after that it expires. We strongly encourage people
to take at least one continuous block of two weeks each year to recover
properly.

Sick leave must be reported from the first day of absence, and a medical
certificate is required from the third day of illness onward. For planned
medical appointments that cannot be scheduled outside working hours, short
absences can be taken without using vacation days, in agreement with the team.

## Travel and Expenses

Business trips are registered in advance in the travel portal. We reimburse
second-class train tickets, economy-class flights, and overnight stays of up to
140 euros per night. For meals there is a daily allowance of 28 euros for trips
longer than eight hours. Receipts must be submitted within 30 days of the trip;
otherwise the claim to reimbursement lapses.

For sustainability reasons, train travel is preferred over short-haul flights
whenever the train journey takes less than five hours. Rental cars and taxis
should only be used where public transport is impractical, and ride-sharing is
encouraged where it does not extend travel time unreasonably.

## Onboarding New Colleagues

New employees go through a structured onboarding during their first four weeks.
On the first day, the laptop, accounts, and the mentoring program are set up. In
the first week there are introductions to the product, the processes, and the
mandatory security training. The detailed task list can be found in the separate
Onboarding Checklist. After 30 days there is a first feedback conversation with
the team lead.

Each new hire is paired with a mentor who is not their direct manager, so that
there is always a low-pressure contact for questions about tools, culture, and
unwritten rules. Managers are responsible for making sure the first real task is
small enough to be finished within the first two weeks.

## Learning and Development

Every employee has an annual learning budget of 1,500 euros for conferences,
courses, books, or certifications. Learning time of up to four hours per week
may be used during working hours, provided it does not conflict with delivery
commitments. Knowledge gained should be shared with the team, for example in
internal talks or short written summaries.

## Performance and Feedback

We run lightweight performance conversations twice a year. They focus on growth,
impact, and goals rather than on ranking people against each other. Feedback is
expected to be continuous: waiting for the formal cycle to raise an issue is
discouraged. Compensation reviews happen once a year and are decoupled from the
day-to-day feedback so that honest conversations are not distorted by money.
